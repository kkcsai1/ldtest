Who demonstrates the Gold Standard?
The increasingly complex and challenging environments in which we operate, demand our best. As executives and leaders, our partners aspire to embody the "Gold Standard" for excellence in delivering exceptional client service, developing our people and achieving the highest levels of quality. We recognize that our stakeholders have high expectations. These ideals are consistent and ambitious. They are not adjusted to apply relative to a partner's tenure, market or service line, but are based on the expectations and aspirations of our entire partner population.

Set goals

Partner goals are based on the Gold Standards that are aligned to the existing global metrics. During goal-setting you will:

  • > Add context to how you will strive toward the Gold Standard
  • > Identify your areas of responsibility for the year ahead
  • > Select your feedback providers
Request feedback

LEAD enables you to request feedback from multiple sources and allows you to add new feedback providers during the year. When requesting feedback, keep in mind that feedback providers:

  • > Should be selected based on your key areas of responsibility and roles
  • > Can be 'weighed' based on your time allocation to that area of responsibility
  • > Require LEAD reviewer approval
  • > Anticipate 3-4 feedback providers in addition to LEAD reviewer
Provide feedback

Feedback is quick and easy to provide and can be submitted multiple times throughout the year. In keeping with the key principles of LEAD, feedback:

  • > Should be open, honest, and constructive
  • > Should be provided in relation to the Gold Standard
  • > Is not anonymous and should be followed up with live discussions

View progress on your personal dashboard
The LEAD dashboard shows how your feedback providers view your performance in relation to the Gold Standard. As the year progresses and more feedback is received, your dashboard provides a real-time view of your performance. You can view your feedback in aggregate, or drill down into the details of each metric. At year-end, once you have completed your self-assessment, you can see your performance in relation to this, as well as your peers. The dashboard is a key enabler of the LEAD conversations that will take place throughout the year; use it to help identify areas for improvement and agree action plans with your LEAD reviewer.
